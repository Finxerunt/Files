Matilyn Wang

Scholar at the International Socioeconomics Laboratory

Matilyn Wang aspires to be a pediatric sports medicine physician. With her previous experience, she hopes to bring more attention to the research of commonly misunderstood topics that often go unaddressed. She would like to address the overlap between issues of socioeconomics and healthcare in many of the problems in today’s world including mental health, and lack of access to healthcare, health insurance, funding, and resources in underserved communities. Matilyn hopes to teach and inspire others to change the world by spreading awareness on these global issues through research and mentoring. From being a tutor to a coach and mentor, Matilyn strives to allow everyone to have the opportunity to bring about long-term solutions for major global issues through researching the overlap between healthcare and socioeconomics in our world. She is grateful for this opportunity to be a part of something greater than herself.

The International Socioeconomics Laboratory™ (ISL) is a global research institute made up of young adults that strives to find adequate solutions to current socio-economic problems. We use existing records and data to create our comprehensive models and studies to find the root cause of these problems and the best ways to fix them.
